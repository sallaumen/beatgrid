was fixed: search now uses **"artist + title"** when an artist tag is present, plus a
**medley confidence guard** (a `/` / pout-pourri / medley name is capped at `:medium`).
The **26 `wrong_match`** tracks were re-resolved (`Soundcharts.re_resolve/1`); ~22 became
**`high` with the correct artist** (Sina → *Djavan*, A Banda → *Chico Buarque*, Azul →

and `verify` flags below remain pending for review; the wrong-match flags were superseded.

## Soundcharts match audit — 60 flagged tracks

**Bottom line:** Do NOT trust an auto-rename pass. Of 60 flagged matches, only **24 (40%) are safe-but-need-a-glance** (`verify`); the other **36 (60%) must be blocked** — 26 are the wrong song entirely and 10 are medleys that would mislabel a single-track file.

### Counts
- **By recommendation:** 26 `wrong_match` · 10 `do_not_rename` (medley) · 24 `verify`
- **By category:** title_mismatch 23 · wrong_artist 11 · truncated_file 11 · medley 10 · other 3 · generic_or_short_title 2
- **By severity:** high 37 · medium 14 · low 9
- **By confidence:** low 45 · high 14 · medium 1

### The dangerous part — false-highs (high confidence, but must NOT be applied)
The matcher's confidence score is unreliable. **3 of the 14 "high"-confidence matches are actually unsafe**, all medleys where the cloud title joins two songs with `/` and renaming would relabel a single-track file:
- `019eff31-1569-...` ` Besame Mucho` → "Mãeana - Aquelas Coisas / Besame Mucho"
- `019eff31-1207-...` ` Insensatez` → "Mãeana - Digo ou Não Digo / Insensatez"
- `019eff31-38cc-...` ` Lavadeira do rio` → "Oswaldinho do Acordeon - Bom forró / Lavadeira do rio"

A 4th deceptive case is **medium**-confidence: `019eff31-308c-...` `Chove Chuva` → a live medley "Por Causa De Você, Menina / Chove Chuva". The remaining 11 "high"-confidence rows are genuine song matches but with duration anomalies (mostly truncated downloads), so they land in `verify`, not auto-apply.

### Headline risk patterns to scan first
1. **Artist-name false hits (highest risk).** The matcher repeatedly latched onto the *artist* string, not the song: `Tarcísio`→"Tarcísio do Acordeon - Voltável", `Foguete`→"Léo Foguete - …", `Mossoró`→"Trio Mossoró - …", `Sina`→"Frank **Sina**tra", `A Banda`→"…**Banda** El Limón…", `Ben`→"**Ben**son Boone". All `wrong_match`.
2. **Cross-genre / cross-language coincidental overlaps.** A forró/MPB library matched to Punjabi (`FOR INDIA`→"PAN INDIA"), Vietnamese (`Chap Chap`→"Chập Chờn"), reggaeton (`Azul`→J Balvin; `A Nível De…`→Myke Towers), Indian film score (`Retrô`→"Kanimaa (From \"Retro\")"), and classical (`Swan Lake`→Tchaikovsky). All wrong song.
3. **Medleys / pot-pourris (the `/` tell).** 10 cloud titles join 2–3 songs with `/`; the file is a single track. Never adopt the medley title — these are `do_not_rename` regardless of confidence.
4. **Generic one-word titles** (`Quero`, `Você`, `Azul`, `Se...`, `Girassol`, `Inverno`) collide easily and dominate the false matches.

### Where the human should start
- **First, lock out the 36 blocked ids** (`do_not_rename_ids` + `wrong_match_ids`) from any auto-rename. Prioritize the 3 false-high medleys and the high-severity wrong-artist hits.
- **Then triage the 24 `verify` rows**, which split into two benign buckets: (a) truncated/short downloads where metadata is right but the audio is incomplete (e.g. `Linha do Equador`, `Partilhar`, `Lá Vai Forró`), and (b) plausible-but-unconfirmed matches needing a listen (`Gostoso Demais`, `Xenhenhem`, `Xodó`). Note `Pagode Russo` (`019eff31-050e-...`): file is ~3x longer than the cloud track — possibly a mislabeled file or concatenated tracks, worth a closer look despite being only `verify`.
